# SECTION 1 — LAUNCH ICP & WEDGE

## 1.1 Why a Wedge Matters

"Indian earners aged 25–50" is the eventual addressable market. It is not the launch audience. A community-first strategy requires a tight first beachhead — a segment where the pain is acute, the identity is strong, the founder has credibility, and word-of-mouth travels fast within the group.

The wedge is where Yes Lifers proves the model before scaling it. Every community ritual, every content piece, every app onboarding decision in Phase 0 and Phase 1 is optimised for this segment. The broader audience comes in Phase 2 and beyond.

## 1.2 The Launch Wedge

**Primary ICP — Phase 0 & 1:**

Urban Indian couples aged roughly 38–48, especially parents with teenage or near-teen children. Typically dual-income or financially established. Based in Tier 1 cities — Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad. Combined household income ₹3L–10L/month. Already investing — SIPs running, some direct equity, insurance in place, possibly real estate. Have done many "right things" financially but do not feel clear. Juggling children's education, aging parents, career transitions, health, retirement, and the desire to live more intentionally. Making life decisions reactively — not because they lack money, but because they lack a framework for weighing competing priorities without guilt.

**Why this segment first:**

| Dimension | Fit |
|---|---|
| Pain intensity | Very high — accumulated responsibility, not early-career uncertainty. These households are not asking "how do I optimise my money?" They are asking "how do we make meaningful life decisions without guilt, confusion, or feeling like we are failing somewhere else?" Every decision feels loaded. The stress comes not from scarcity but from competing demands on a finite life. |
| Identity readiness | They have been responsible for years and want permission to reclaim life. The Yes Lifer identity speaks to who they want to become, not who they already are. "You have carried a lot — let's bring clarity to what comes next" resonates because it validates their effort while opening a new chapter. |
| Founder credibility | House of Alpha's advisory client base skews toward this demographic. The SEBI registration, financial planning methodology, and advisory track record feel most authentic and credible with this audience — because they are this audience. |
| Distribution density | Concentrated in LinkedIn professional networks, school parent communities, corporate wellness circles, and Tier 1 city social networks. Parents talk to parents — referral density within life-stage peer groups is very high. Corporate "financial wellness for working parents" workshops provide a high-trust introduction channel. |
| Willingness to pay | ₹2,999–₹4,999 is trivial at this income level. The barrier is trust, not price. Community solves trust. This cohort is also more likely to convert to advisory (₹4,999+) because the complexity of their situation genuinely warrants it. |
| Decision urgency | This cohort is actively facing simultaneous Hell Yes! Decisions — children's higher education funding (₹15L–₹50L+ in 3–7 years), second-innings career planning, retirement clarity, aging parent care, deferred travel and lifestyle goals. The app's scenario modelling is immediately relevant because the trade-offs are real and urgent. |

**Secondary ICP — Phase 2 expansion:**

Younger professionals and couples in Tier 1 cities. Age 28–38. ₹2L–6L/month income. Dual-income or single high earner. Early career to growing family. The "Arjun & Meera" and "Nisha" profiles from the app. The community proof, content library, and brand credibility from Phase 1 make this segment reachable — and the aspiration flows naturally: younger earners look at the Yes Lifer parents and see who they want to become.

## 1.3 Wedge-Specific Messaging

The core Yes Lifers messaging applies universally, but the lead messages for Phase 0–1 content lean into the wedge's specific tensions:

| Tension | Message angle | Why it lands with the wedge |
|---|---|---|
| Responsibility ≠ clarity | "You've been responsible for 15 years. When did you last ask what it's all for?" | Validates their effort while naming the gap. These couples have done the right things but don't feel calm or clear. |
| Competing timelines | "Your child's 11th-grade board exams and your retirement land in the same decade. Have you planned for both?" | Specific, urgent, and emotionally loaded. Two non-deferrable goals competing for the same pool of money. |
| Deferred living | "You delayed that trip again. Not because you can't afford it — because it felt irresponsible." | Names the guilt that prevents intentional living despite financial capacity. The core Yes Lifer tension for this cohort. |
| Accumulated overload | "You've made every right financial move. Why doesn't it feel calm yet?" | Directly addresses the emotional paradox — doing everything right but still feeling uncertain. |
| Education funding anxiety | "Your child is 13. You have 5 years to fund their dream. Do you know the number?" | Hyper-specific to the wedge life stage. Creates urgency without fear — invites clarity. |
| Duty vs desire | "You don't have to choose between being a responsible parent and living a meaningful life." | The brand promise distilled into one line. Permission-giving, not prescriptive. |

## 1.4 Wedge Validation Criteria

Before expanding beyond the wedge (Phase 2), these must be true:

- 70%+ of Phase 1 community members match the primary ICP profile (38–48, parents, urban)
- Message testing shows >3% engagement on wedge-specific content (parental tension, duty vs desire, competing timelines — not just broad philosophical content)
- At least 5 organic community referrals from within parent/professional networks (not from content — from member-to-member)
- Quiz completion data shows the wedge segment has meaningfully higher completion rates than other segments
- At least 3 community members have brought a Hell Yes! Decision to the community that maps to the app's decision categories (education funding, retirement clarity, second-innings planning, aging parent support)

If these are not true by Week 12, the wedge hypothesis is wrong and must be revised before Phase 2.

# SECTION 3 — COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

## 3.1 Why This Section Exists

Yes Lifers sits adjacent to regulated financial advice. House of Alpha is a SEBI Registered Investment Adviser. The community, social content, testimonials, and advisory upsell path all operate in a space where SEBI regulations apply. The red-team feedback correctly identifies this as the most glaring omission in the original GTM.

This framework is drafted from first principles as a working reference. **It must be reviewed and blessed by a qualified compliance advisor before any public-facing content is published.** The questions at the end of each subsection are the specific items the compliance advisor needs to answer.

## 3.2 SEBI Regulatory Context

The following regulations are relevant to the Yes Lifers GTM:

- **SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013 (as amended)** — governs what an IA can and cannot say, how advice is delivered, and fee structures
- **SEBI Master Circular for Investment Advisers (February 6, 2026)** — consolidated guidance on IA conduct, advertising, client onboarding
- **SEBI Circular on Social Media Disclosure (February 26, 2026)** — requires registered entities and their agents to disclose registered name and registration number on social media platforms
- **SEBI Advertisement Code for IAs** — governs claims, testimonials, and promotional content

## 3.3 Social Media Disclosure Requirements

**Mandatory on every platform where the founder or House of Alpha posts:**

- Registered name: [House of Alpha's SEBI-registered legal name]
- SEBI Registration Number: [INA number]
- This must appear in the bio/about section of every social platform used for GTM (LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X)

**Per-post disclosure:**

- Any post that references financial planning, investment strategy, or advice must include the registration disclosure — either in the post body or as a standard footer

**Questions for compliance advisor:**
- Does the SEBI Feb 2026 circular require disclosure on every individual post, or is a bio/profile-level disclosure sufficient?
- Does the founder's personal LinkedIn account require disclosure if they post about Yes Lifers, or only the company page?
- Do community manager posts inside Circle require disclosure?
- If a community member shares a Yes Lifers post, does the reshare need disclosure?

## 3.4 Approved Claims Library

Content published under the Yes Lifers or House of Alpha name must use only approved claims. The following framework categorises what can and cannot be said.

**ALWAYS ALLOWED — Educational & philosophical content:**

- General financial literacy (what is an emergency fund, how SIPs work, what term insurance does)
- The Yes Lifers philosophy (living well today + building wealth, guilt-free spending, intentional money)
- The 7-step journey as a framework (these are not advice — they are a personal development methodology)
- Statistics and data about financial behaviour (average investor underperformance, protection gap statistics, asset allocation research) — with source citation
- The Yes Budget concept as a budgeting philosophy (not as personalised advice)

**ALLOWED WITH DISCLOSURE — Product and service claims:**

- "SEBI Registered Investment Adviser" — must include registration number
- "Personalised financial plan" — allowed, but must not imply guaranteed outcomes
- Pricing (₹2,999 / ₹4,999 / implementation support) — allowed with full fee structure disclosed
- "Complimentary advisor call" — allowed

**NEVER ALLOWED — Prohibited content:**

- Specific return promises ("earn 15% returns", "double your money")
- Performance guarantees ("your retirement is guaranteed", "you will reach ₹X crore")
- Specific fund or stock recommendations in public content (this is advice, not education)
- Comparison with other advisors/platforms that implies superiority without substantiation
- Any claim that could be construed as personalised investment advice delivered outside the regulated advisory relationship
- Urgency language that implies market timing ("invest now before the crash", "markets are about to rally")

**CAUTION ZONE — Requires case-by-case compliance review:**

- Member testimonials (see 3.5)
- "What Could Be" scenarios from the app (these use specific numbers — are they advice?)
- Hell Yes! Decision community discussions (when does a community conversation become advice?)
- Profile card stories (Rajesh, Nisha, etc.) — are these compliant if they include specific figures?
- Before/after comparisons ("corpus without plan vs with plan")

**Questions for compliance advisor:**
- Can we use the "What Could Be" card language (with specific corpus numbers) in public marketing, or only inside the app after data collection?
- Do the profile card stories (Rajesh, Arjun & Meera, etc.) with their specific numbers constitute hypothetical illustrations under SEBI rules, and if so, what disclaimers are required?
- Can we show before/after retirement scenarios in social content, or only in the personalised plan output?
- Is the statement "the average investor underperforms by 3–4% annually" an approved claim if sourced, or does it require a specific SEBI-format disclaimer?

## 3.5 Testimonial Rules

Testimonials are the highest-leverage GTM content and the highest-compliance-risk content.

**Working rules (to be confirmed by compliance advisor):**

- Members may share their experience of the process (how the plan felt, what clarity they gained, the emotional impact) — this is experiential, not advice
- Members may NOT share specific returns, portfolio performance, or fund recommendations
- Members may NOT imply that their results are typical or guaranteed for others
- Every testimonial used in marketing must include a disclaimer: "Individual experiences may vary. Past results are not indicative of future performance."
- Video testimonials must include the disclaimer in the video description and ideally spoken/displayed
- Community "Say Yes moments" and "Clarity moments" are experiential shares, not testimonials — but if used in external marketing, the same rules apply
- Screenshots of community conversations used in marketing must be approved by the member AND reviewed for compliance

**The community share moment post template:**

The pre-written share template ("I just completed Step 1 of my Yes Lifer journey. I finally know where I stand.") is designed to be experiential, not numerical. This is intentional. **The share template must never prompt members to share specific numbers, returns, or fund names.**

**Questions for compliance advisor:**
- Under SEBI's advertisement code, can client testimonials be used at all in IA marketing? If so, what specific format and disclaimers are required?
- Do community posts inside a private Circle group count as "advertising" under SEBI rules, or are they member-to-member communication?
- If a member voluntarily posts their corpus numbers in the community, and another member screenshots and shares externally, what is House of Alpha's liability?
- Can we use anonymised case studies (the profile card format) in marketing if we add a disclaimer that these are illustrative?

## 3.6 The Education-to-Advice Boundary

This is the most critical compliance distinction in the GTM. The community, content, and free app output are education. The paid plan and advisory are regulated advice. The boundary must be bright, visible, and consistently enforced.

**Education (community, content, free app):**

- General principles of financial planning
- Frameworks for decision-making (the 7 steps, the Yes Budget concept, the Hell Yes! framework)
- The free financial snapshot (Stage 4 in the app) — this shows the user's picture, not a recommendation
- Community discussions about financial decisions — as long as no specific product/fund/amount is recommended for a specific person's situation

**Advice (paid plan, investment advisory, implementation):**

- Any personalised recommendation (specific funds, specific allocation, specific SIP amounts for a specific user)
- Portfolio analysis with buy/sell/hold recommendations
- Tax optimisation recommendations specific to a user's situation
- Any "you should do X" statement directed at a specific user's financial situation

**Where the boundary gets tested:**

| Scenario | Education or advice? | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Community member asks "should I buy a house?" and members discuss | Education — if discussion stays at framework level ("here's how to think about it") | Moderator redirects if anyone says "yes, buy the house at ₹X" |
| Founder posts "here's how I think about emergency funds" | Education | Allowed |
| Founder posts "if you earn ₹2L/month, you need a ₹6L emergency fund" | Caution zone — this is general but specific enough to feel like advice | Add disclaimer: "This is a general guideline, not personalised advice" |
| Community member shares their plan output numbers and asks for feedback | Advice risk — members commenting on specific numbers crosses the line | Moderator redirects: "Great question for your advisor call" |
| App's free output shows "your term cover gap is ₹1.25 Cr" | Advice — this is a personalised finding | This is permitted because it's inside the regulated IA relationship (user has engaged with the app) — confirm with compliance |
| Hell Yes! Decision community thread — member presents a real scenario with real numbers | High risk | Anonymise the numbers. Frame as "how would you think about this" not "what should this person do." Moderator watches actively. |

**Community moderation protocol for the boundary:**

The community manager must be trained on this boundary. The protocol:

1. If a community post or comment crosses from education into advice, the community manager replies: "Great question — and one that really deserves personalised analysis. Have you tried the Hell Yes! Decision tool in the app, or would a free advisor call help?"
2. The reply is warm, not policing. It redirects, not restricts.
3. If a member repeatedly provides specific financial advice to others, DM them privately: "We love your knowledge — but specific recommendations put us in a regulatory grey zone. Could you frame it as 'here's how I thought about it' rather than 'you should do X'?"
4. Escalate to the compliance reviewer if a post is clearly advice (specific fund names, specific amounts for a specific person).

**Questions for compliance advisor:**
- Does the free app snapshot (Stage 4 output — showing gaps and blind spots but not specific recommendations) constitute advice under SEBI regulations?
- When a SEBI-registered IA operates a community, are all community interactions considered "advice" by default, or only those that meet the definition of personalised recommendation?
- Can the community manager (who is not a registered IA) moderate financial discussions, or must all financial content in the community be reviewed by a registered person?
- What is the liability if a community member gives bad advice to another member inside the Yes Lifers community?
- Should the community terms of service include a specific waiver/disclosure about SEBI regulation?

### 4.23 The Flywheel

Post-launch, the three layers operate as a self-reinforcing loop:

1. Community members → app users (trust → adoption)
2. App users share milestones → community (progress → social proof)
3. Community proof → new members (organic growth)
4. Power app users → advisory upgrade (high-value conversion)
5. Advisory clients → transformation stories in community (ultimate social proof)

**The flywheel is the strategy. If it's spinning, scale spend on what feeds it. If it's not spinning, stop everything and diagnose which link is broken.**

### 4.24 Retention Mechanics

**In-App:**

- Milestone nudges: app detects milestones (emergency fund hit, SIP streak, goal progress) → one-tap community share prompt
- Step progression: dashboard evolves with the member's journey. Step 1 users see different prompts than Step 5 users.
- Annual re-reflection: 12-month mark → full re-reflection flow → "Year in Review" card shareable on social
- Event-based nudges: life events (salary change, job switch) → "Your life just changed. Let's update your Yes Life plan."

**Community:**

- Graduated leadership: Step 6–7 members invited to lead pods, host Clarity Circles, moderate channels
- Quarterly "Yes Life Awards": community-voted awards for best transformation, most helpful member, boldest Hell Yes! Decision
- Content from within: by Month 6, target 30%+ of community content from members

### 4.25 Advisory Upsell Path

The advisory conversion must feel like the natural next step, not a sales pitch.

**Trigger 1 — Complexity:** App detects a scenario too complex for self-service → "This is a Hell Yes! Decision that deserves a human advisor."

**Trigger 2 — Implementation:** After plan delivery, user hasn't acted in 14 days → "Want us to implement your Yes Pathway?"

**Trigger 3 — Social proof:** Advisory clients share anonymised results in community → organic inquiry from other members.

**Handoff mechanics:**

| Step | Owner | SLA |

# SECTION 5 — CONTENT ENGINE

## 5.1 Content Strategy — The Two Jobs

Every piece of content serves one or both of two jobs:

1. **Attract:** Bring new people into the top of the funnel (waitlist → community → app)
2. **Arm:** Give existing members shareable assets that drive organic referrals

Content is not brand awareness. It is a conversion mechanism. Every post should have a clear path to the next step (quiz, waitlist, community join, app download).

## 5.2 LinkedIn Content System

**Frequency:** 3–4 posts/week
**Voice:** Founder's personal voice (ghostwritten by GTM, founder reviews and publishes)
**Format rotation:**

### Monday — Philosophy Post

**Purpose:** Core tension framing. Thought leadership. The "why" behind Yes Lifers.
**Format:** Long-form text (800–1,200 words) OR short-form with a hook (200–400 words)
**Tone:** Reflective, slightly contrarian, warm
**Structure:**
1. Hook — one provocative line that stops the scroll
2. Tension — the problem stated honestly
3. Reframe — the Yes Lifers perspective
4. Close — one sentence that invites reflection, not action

**Content brief template — Philosophy post:**

```
HOOK: [One sentence — provocative, personal, or counterintuitive]
TENSION: [2–3 sentences — the problem as the audience experiences it]
REFRAME: [3–5 sentences — the Yes Lifers perspective. Not advice. A way of seeing.]
CLOSE: [One question or statement that sits with the reader]
CTA: [Soft — "If this resonates, I'm building a community around this idea. Link in bio."]
COMPLIANCE: [No specific financial recommendations. No return promises. Educational/philosophical only.]
```

**Example posts (wedge-optimised):**

Post 1: "You've been responsible for 15 years. SIPs running. Insurance in place. Kids in good schools. Career on track. When did you last ask what it's all for? Not 'am I saving enough.' But — what am I building toward? Most parents I speak to can tell you their child's board exam date. Ask them when they plan to retire and you'll get a shrug. The plan isn't missing because you're careless. It's missing because no one taught responsible people how to want things without guilt."

Post 2: "Guilt-free spending is a financial outcome, not a personality trait. You can engineer it. It starts with knowing — with certainty — that your child's education is funded, your retirement SIP is running, your protection is in place, and your parents are covered. After that? The family trip, the anniversary dinner, the home upgrade — it's not irresponsible. It's the entire point. You didn't work this hard to feel guilty about a holiday."

Post 3: "Your daughter turns 18 in 5 years. You want to retire in 12. Your parents need support now. Three timelines. One income. Zero framework. Most families I work with have done everything right — SIPs, term cover, PPF, even some direct equity. But they've never sat down and asked: can all three of these things happen? And if not, what do we trade? That's not a spreadsheet question. That's a life design question."

| Parameter | Estimate |

## 6.4 Pricing Validation

The ₹2,999 and ₹4,999 price points are set. Validate them:

- Monitor conversion rates at each price point weekly
- If Option 1 conversion is <10%, test a lower entry point (₹1,999) or a time-limited offer

- Consider annual pricing for ongoing advisory (₹9,999/year or similar) vs one-time

# SECTION 8 — FUNNEL CONTROL SYSTEM

## 8.1 The Five Numbers That Matter

Every weekly GTM review begins with these five numbers. They are the control system for the entire GTM.

| # | Metric | What it tells you | Where it comes from |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Top of funnel — new leads this week** | Is the content engine working? Are people finding us? | Beehiiv (email signups) + Typeform (quiz completions) |
| 2 | **Community — WAU this week** | Is the community alive? Are people engaging? | Circle dashboard |
| 3 | **Conversion — community members who started the app this week** | Is the bridge working? Are people moving from trust to action? | App analytics (referral tag) |
| 4 | **Activation — snapshots completed this week** | Are app users getting value? Is the onboarding working? | MixPanel |
| 5 | **Revenue — plans purchased this week** | Are we making money? Is the value proposition landing? | Razorpay/Stripe |

These five numbers cascade: if #1 drops, #3 will drop in 2–4 weeks. If #3 drops but #1 is fine, the bridge is broken. If #4 drops but #3 is fine, the app onboarding has friction. Each number isolates a different part of the system.

## 8.3 Stage Gate Decision Rules

The stage gates in Section 4 (Phase playbooks) define the thresholds. Here is how to use them:

**GREEN:** Metric is at or above target. No action needed. Keep cadence.

**AMBER:** Metric is below target but above red threshold. **Action required this week.** The stage gate prescribes the specific action — follow it. Do not wait to see if it recovers on its own. Log the action taken.

**RED:** Metric is significantly below target. **Stop and diagnose before continuing.** A red metric means something structural is wrong — not just a slow week. The stage gate prescribes the diagnostic action. If a metric stays red for 2 consecutive weeks, escalate to the founder and consider pausing the current phase.

**Phase transition rule:** A phase does not end until all exit criteria are met. If exit criteria are not met by the target week, the phase extends. No exceptions. Launching the app into an underbuilt community wastes the entire community-first thesis.

## 8.4 Kill Criteria

These are the conditions under which the strategy itself — not just a tactic — needs fundamental revision:

| Condition | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Wedge is wrong | <40% of community matches ICP by Week 12 AND organic referrals from parent/professional networks = 0 | Stop. Redefine ICP. Restart Phase 1 content with new wedge. |
| Community model doesn't work | WAU <20% for 4 consecutive weeks despite interventions | Community is not providing enough value. Survey members. Consider a different community format or value prop. |
| App bridge is broken | Community → app conversion <10% after 4 weeks of Phase 2 | The trust isn't transferring to the product. Major app or onboarding revision needed. |
| Revenue model doesn't work | Paid conversion <3% after 8 weeks of Phase 2 | Value of paid plan not clear. Consider freemium pivot, pricing change, or product revision. |
| Founder burnout | Founder misses >50% of commitments for 2 consecutive weeks | Immediate delegation review. Reduce founder scope. Hire or reassign. |
