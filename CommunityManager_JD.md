**Community Manager — Yes Lifers**

**Start:** Week 5 of GTM (Phase 1 launch). Part-time initially (15–20 hrs/week), scaling to full-time when community exceeds 500 members or daily moderation exceeds 2 hrs/day.

**What this role does in one line:** Owns the daily heartbeat of the Yes Lifers community — the person who makes 2,000 members feel welcomed, heard, and moving forward.

**Skillsets needed:**

The person must be naturally warm in text — the kind of person who writes DMs that feel personal, not templated. They need to be comfortable holding space in conversations about money without giving financial advice, which is a specific and trainable but non-obvious skill. They should be able to read a thread and sense when someone is venting (let them), asking for frameworks (engage), or asking for specific advice (redirect). That boundary instinct is the single most important skill for this role given SEBI regulations.

Beyond that: comfort with Circle (or willingness to learn fast), basic content sense (can they write a compelling daily prompt, not just "share your wins!"), ability to track pods and follow up without nagging, and enough organisational discipline to run a weekly rhythm without supervision. They don't need to be a finance expert — they need to be a community expert who learns the Yes Lifers vocabulary.

**Responsibilities:**

Daily operations — post the Say Yes Moment prompt every morning, welcome new members with a personalised DM within 24 hours of joining, respond to posts and keep conversations alive, monitor all channels for engagement quality and compliance red flags.

Onboarding — guide every new member through the entry flow: welcome DM → prompt to post in #introductions using the template → suggest first channel based on their intro → at Day 7, prompt to join a pod.

Accountability pods — recruit members into pods, match them by life stage and goals, facilitate weekly check-ins for all active pods (until veteran members are trained as facilitators around Month 4), track pod completion and coordinate public celebrations.

Rituals — facilitate weekly Clarity Circles (the founder hosts one per month, the community manager runs the other three), post bi-weekly Hell Yes! Decision threads using the structured template, coordinate the monthly retrospective with the GTM team.

Moderation & compliance — actively manage the #hell-yes-decisions channel where the education-to-advice boundary is most at risk. When a post crosses from "here's how I think about it" into "you should do X with your money," redirect warmly: "Great question — the Hell Yes! Decision tool in the app or a free advisor call can help with personalised analysis." DM repeat offenders privately. Escalate genuinely problematic content to the GTM team. Run a weekly moderation scan and flag any compliance concerns.

Community-to-app bridge — identify members showing conversion signals (completed Step 1 discussions, shared a Hell Yes! Decision, finished a pod sprint) and send personalised DMs with app invites at the right moment. Track which triggers convert and feed that back to the GTM team.

Advisory lead flagging — when a member signals advisory-level need (complexity beyond self-service, asks for implementation help, directly asks about working with an advisor), flag them in the CRM within 24 hours so the GTM team can qualify and hand off.

Reporting — provide weekly community health inputs for the Monday GTM review: WAU count, new members, pod activity, moderation flags, notable threads, member sentiment. This takes 15 minutes and feeds directly into the GTM Operating System.

**What this role does NOT do:**

Does not create LinkedIn or Instagram content (that's the content producer and GTM team). Does not give financial advice — ever. Does not make compliance decisions on ambiguous cases (escalates them). Does not manage the founder's schedule or content approvals. Does not own metrics or stage gate decisions.

**Graduated leadership path:**

By Month 4, the community manager should be training 3–5 veteran members (Step 6–7 in their journey) to co-facilitate pods and Clarity Circles. By Month 6, the community manager's role shifts from "doing everything" to "running the community team" — facilitators handle pods, the CM handles onboarding, moderation, and quality.
